Anytop: Character Animation Diffusion with Any Topology

Under review
Paper Code Video

Anytop generates motions for diverse characters with distinct motion dynamics, using only their skeletal structure as an input.

Abstract

Generating motion for arbitrary skeletons is a longstanding challenge in computer graphics, remaining largely unexplored due to the scarcity of diverse datasets and the irregular nature of the data. In this work, we introduce AnyTop, a diffusion model that generates motions for diverse characters with distinct motion dynamics, using only their skeletal structure as input. Our work features a transformer-based denoising network, tailored for arbitrary skeleton learning, integrating topology information into the traditional attention mechanism. Additionally, by incorporating textual joint descriptions into the latent feature representation, AnyTop learns semantic demonstrates that AnyTop generalizes well, even with as few as three training examples per topology, and can produce motions for unseen skeletons as well. Furthermore, our model's latent space is highly informative, enabling downstream tasks such as joint correspondence and temporal segmentation.

Overview

The input to AnyTop is a noised motion \( X_t\) and the skeleton \(S = { \mathcal{P}_\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{R}_\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{D}_\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{N}_\mathcal{S} }\), where \(\mathcal{P}_\mathcal{S} \) is the rest-pose, \(\mathcal{R}_\mathcal{S}\) is the joints relations, \(\mathcal{D}_\mathcal{S}\) is the topological distances between each pair of joints and \(\mathcal{N}_\mathcal{S}\) is the joints names. The Enrichment Block incorporates the skeletal features into the noised motion by concatenating the embedded \(\mathcal{P}_\mathcal{S}\) to the sequence and adding a T5-embedded name to each joint. The enriched motion is then passed through a stack of L Skeletal Temporal Transformer layers. We apply skeletal attention along the joint axis, capturing interactions between all joints, and incorporate the topology information \(\mathcal{R}_\mathcal{S}\) and \(\mathcal{D}_\mathcal{S}\) to attention maps. Next, we apply temporal attention along the frames axis. Finally, the output is projected back to the motion features dimension, facilitating the reconstruction of the motion sequence.

Correspondence in latent space

Anytop cross-skeleton manifold captures \(\textit{spatial}\) and \(\textit{temporal}\) correspondence, where functionally similar body parts and analogous actions and poses share similar latent representations.
